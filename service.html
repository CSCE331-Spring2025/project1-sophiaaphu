Service

Overall, serving my community has been a significant part of my time as an Aggie Engineer. Through service, I am able to help my community of fellow Aggie Engineers and potentially give someone the support that they need to succeed.

CSCE 120 Undergraduate TA

I have served as a TA for CSCE 120 since Fall 2024. Overall it has been a valuable and enjoyable experience. I enjoy getting to teach students vital C++ concepts that set the foundation for later C++ classes. I always am happy getting to lead my lab section every week (even if it is at 8am this semester) and help students with their homework in office hours.

I also found the opportunity to coordinate course materials a insightful experience. I was able to learn how a class is managed logistically from setting up assignments, grading exams, to even detecting plagiarism and artificial intelligence use. Though TAing has been a lot of work, I wouldn't trade the experience for the world and intend on TAing till graduation.

Engineering TA Organization President

When I was a new freshman engineer, I was trying to find a supportive community that would help aggie engineers through their time as ETAMing freshman. I found the Engineering TAO, and becoming involved in it was one of the best decisions I made. It was one of the most active and supportive communities I joined, and I enjoy the opportunity to provide support to ETAMing freshman and other engineers in the server.

I joined up as a web officer in February 2024 and helped TAO develop their website. This opportunity helped me hone my web development skills and collaborate as a team to create a cool product. As president, I have been able to coordinate cool opportunities that would improve TAO for its members like hosting a Big Tech Intern Panel, a resume review event, creating new web applications and internal tools, and continuing to foster the wonderful community of TAO.

Aggie Coding Club Projects Officer

As a freshman engineer, I was looking for opportunities to get involved in meaningful projects, connect with fellow computer science and engineering majors, and learn more about starting my career. I found Aggie Coding Club to be a great place to grow my coding skills and work on practical projects alongside other students.

Joining as a projects officer in February 2024 allowed me to help coordinate hackathons, mentor new members, and lead development efforts for club projects. It has been a rewarding experience seeing students grow and develop their skills in coding, teamwork, and problem-solving.
